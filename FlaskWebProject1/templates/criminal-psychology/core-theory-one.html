Social Learning Theory

Explanation

This theory says that all behaviour is learnt and that it begins with a role model; this is usually someone you look up to and respect.
Once someone finds a role model, they may go through a stage called identification in which they decide they want to be like the person they look up to and begin a process of observation and imitation to internalise their behaviour.
Once a behaviour is internalised it will soon become habitual meaning that people may engage in the behaviour regardless of the consequences.

Why do people try to imitate their role models?

It is to do with vicarious reinforcement; it describes what happens when we see others being rewarded for their behaviour decide we want the same rewards and that immitating their behaviour will get us them.
The behaviour is strengethened when we experience direct reinforcememt which is when the person imitating the behaviour recieves a similar reward.

Limitations

  1. It ignores the role of nature
  2. Doesn't explain how criminal behaviour started in the first place
  3. Dosen't explain why people turn to crime without criminal role models
  4. Dosen't explain why there is so much crime; if behaviour can be reinforced with rewards shouldn't it be reduced punishment? So why do people commit crimes dispite seeing the negative consequences?
